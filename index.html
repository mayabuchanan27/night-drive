NIGHT DRIVE

A dark Hawaiian supernatural horror game inspired by Pele, Menehune, Night Marchers, and the isolated roads of Oʻahu.

Game Pitch

NIGHT DRIVE is a supernatural 2D top-down horror adventure game set on the dark roads on the east side of Oʻahu. The player, playing as Kiele, unknowingly encounters Pele during a late-night drive home, leading her into a dangerous journey through the isolated haunted location of Waimānalo, where her car breaks down. Kiele must survive encounters with Menehune and Night Marchers as she searches for repair parts to escape and help Pele return to her home.

The game combines exploration, survival, suspense, and mini-game combat encounters while creating an eerie atmosphere inspired by local ghost stories and Hawaiian mythology.

Story & Gameplay

Introduction

The game begins with the player driving home late at night after sunset on Oʻahu. The road is quiet and the sky slowly fades from orange to dark.

At first everything seems normal. The radio plays softly and the headlights cut through the empty road. As the player drives farther, the surroundings begin to feel unfamiliar.

The GPS signal fades. Road signs stop making sense. Without realizing it, the player ends up on the dark road of Makapuʻu near Waimānalo.

A woman dressed in white suddenly appears in the headlights.

The player must choose whether to let her into the vehicle or drive away.

If the player chooses to stop, the woman quietly enters the vehicle and sits silently in the back seat. Not long after, the car suddenly breaks down on a dark stretch of road in Waimānalo. Smoke rises from the hood as the engine dies.

The woman reveals herself as Pele and warns Kiele that the Menehune nearby do not want her traveling through the area.

Gameplay

After the car breaks down, the game transitions from cinematic storytelling into active gameplay.

The player takes control of Kiele and must explore the surrounding area searching for missing car parts hidden throughout the map.

While exploring, Kiele must avoid or survive encounters with Menehune hiding throughout the environment. Some Menehune appear alone while others are stronger enemies such as the Menehune Leader.

Along the road, Kiele may discover a food truck that supplies bentos, musubi, and useful tools that can help during encounters.

When encountering Menehune, the player is given multiple choices:

  • Fight the Menehune
  • Compliment or taunt them
  • Trade tools or items
  • Offer bentos or musubi
  • Run away

Different choices lead to different outcomes. If the wrong action is chosen, the Menehune may become hostile and attack Kiele, causing her health bar to drop.

The player must carefully balance survival, exploration, and interaction while searching for all the missing car parts.

Level Conclusion

After recovering the final missing seat belt piece and surviving the Menehune, Kiele returns safely to the vehicle.

The level concludes with Kiele defeating the Menehune threat and continuing the journey deeper into the night.

Future Development

Future versions of the game would expand beyond repeated car breakdown scenarios by introducing:

  • Multiple endings based on player decisions
  • Expanded driving mechanics
  • Dialogue choices
  • More explorable locations across Oʻahu
  • Additional Hawaiian supernatural encounters
  • Improved animations and sound design
  • Voice acting and cinematic cutscenes
