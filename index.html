NIGHT DRIVE

A dark Hawaiian supernatural horror game inspired by Pele, Menehune, Night Marchers, and the isolated roads of Oʻahu.

Game Pitch

NIGHT DRIVE is a supernatural horror adventure game set on the dark roads of Oʻahu. The player unknowingly encounters Pele during a late-night drive, leading them into a dangerous journey through isolated locations haunted by Hawaiian spirits and legends.

The game combines exploration, survival, suspense, and mini-game combat encounters while creating an eerie atmosphere inspired by local ghost stories and Hawaiian mythology.

Story & Gameplay

Introduction

The game begins with the player driving home late at night after sunset on Oʻahu. The road is quiet and the sky slowly fades from orange to dark.

At first everything seems normal. The radio plays softly and the headlights cut through the empty road. As the player drives farther, the surroundings begin to feel unfamiliar.

The GPS signal fades. Road signs stop making sense. Without realizing it, the player ends up on the dark road of Makapuʻu near Waimānalo.

A woman dressed in white suddenly appears in the headlights.

The player must choose whether to let her into the vehicle or drive away.

First Mini Game

The car suddenly breaks down in Waimānalo. Pele explains that Menehune nearby do not want her traveling through the area.

The player must search for a missing engine part while defending against Menehune emerging from the forest shadows.

Second Mini Game

The car breaks down again at another isolated location. This time the player must survive encounters with Night Marchers while searching for repair parts.

Final Encounter

During the final breakdown, the Menehune return and merge together into a giant creature.

The player must defend Pele and survive the final encounter before escaping the island road.

Design & Illustrations

The game uses dark environments, volcanic lighting, fog, forests, abandoned roads, and eerie audio design to create tension and suspense.

Future Development

Future versions of the game would expand beyond repeated car breakdown scenarios by introducing:

  • Multiple endings based on player decisions
  • Expanded driving mechanics
  • Dialogue choices
  • More explorable locations across Oʻahu
  • Additional Hawaiian supernatural encounters
  • Improved animations and sound design
  • Voice acting and cinematic cutscenes
